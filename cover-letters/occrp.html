A Good Fit to Fight Crime?

Reading this job description took me back to pre-adolescent mornings when I would wake to the clear, confident tones of BBC reporters as rebroadcast by National Public Radio here in America. I lay half-dreaming the news of a world far beyond my own, often scandal or disaster or as much impending, couched in complex political realities and spoken with that distinct air of objectivity and moral consequence.

My father was a journalist early in his career, and while he instilled in me a value of political awareness, I found myself disillusioned by what I learned as a teenager about the influence of corporations in American politics via campaign financing, revolving-door regulatory bodies, and woefully centralized media outlets. Despite hewing from politics and media in my career, I’ve always valued independent journalism and its ability to show us where our social reality falls short of our political values.

That tack perhaps has finally brought me to a point of working in service to the Fourth Estate.

Over the last 20+ years, I’ve developed an unusually broad skillset in software product development:

  • At Google, I wrangled an 8TB dataset of user interaction data from Facebook games to build the friend-recommender service for Google+.
  • At IRL, I maintained a ~5 million DAU MySQL instance and coordinated across teams to develop a plan for 10x growth.
  • At Aura, I increased development velocity by writing tests for the LLM and prompts used to power a family documentarian app.
  • At university, I used tf-idf vectorization and cosine similarity to build a note-taking app that allowed students to discover notes similar to their own.
  • At ReSim, I course-corrected the product by recognizing parallels between the workflow of their customers, robotics developers, and consumer web software engineers.
  • At Aikito, I managed developers across a 13-hour timezone span, hired and mentored an intern, and ran weekly product meetings.

I’ve also completed two machine learning courses (Google’s online course and Deep Atlas’ in-person), worked regularly with infrastructure in AWS, and built tools for the design teams of WhatsApp, Instagram, and Facebook.

My favorite roles have always been those that require me to work beyond engineering, aiming at organizational outcomes with good user experience design and the right technical tradeoffs. It would be a delight and an honor to discuss bringing my skills to the OCCRP.
